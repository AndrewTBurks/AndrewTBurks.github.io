Bio

My name is Andrew Burks.

I am currently pursuing a BS in Computer Science at the University of Illinois at Chicago (UIC) and I am planning to graduate in Dec. 2017. While working towards my degree, I have been working at the Electronic Visualization Laboratory (EVL), participating in the National Science Foundation's (NSF) Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU) program.

My research interests include Data Visualization/Visual Analytics and Human Computer Interaction.

About Me

My Work

Through this work, I have gained hands-on experience in two main areas: Data Visualization and Visual Analytics, and Human Computer Interaction. The majority of my work goes towards harnessing the latest in web-based technologies to create an accessible experience for any user.

In the field of Data Visualization and Visual Analytics, I have experience working with diverse data sources as well as familiarity with cross-disciplinary collaboration. In past projects, I have tackled problems in the fields of:

  • Computational Fluid Dynamics (Viscous Fingers)
  • Computational Biology (Protein-Protein Interactions & Stochastic Gene Regulatory Networks)
  • Precison Medicine
  • Urban Policy
  • and Social Service Equity

Aside for Data Visualization, I was funded to work on the $5M NSF grant for SAGE2: Scalable Amplified Group Environment. Working on SAGE2, I developed a "Partition" system allow users to have more options to organize their content. Additionally, I worked on maintaning and improving core functionality of the software. Through this work, I learned about the interplay between client(s) and server on cross-client applications for large-displays.
